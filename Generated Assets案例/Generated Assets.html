📋 Company Overview 1 / 3

A Shift in Investment Access

Generated Assets represents a fundamental shift in how everyday investors access customized portfolio management. Rather than choosing from a limited menu of ETFs or hiring expensive advisors, users articulate their investment thesis in plain English, and AI agents research, filter, and assemble diversified indices in minutes. This democratization transforms institutional-grade portfolio construction from a gatekept activity into a consumer-grade experience.

Core Mission: Democratize institutional-grade portfolio management through AI, enabling every investor to turn conviction into diversified returns.

Launched in November 2025 by Public.com, Generated Assets has rapidly scaled to 2.5M+ active portfolios and $4.2B in AUM—proof that there is massive demand for personalized, thematic investing outside the constraints of traditional ETFs.

From Vision to Reality

Public.com's leadership recognized an opportunity to leverage recent advances in large language models and agentic AI to solve a structural problem: traditional investing platforms offered limited customization. Investors were confined to pre-built ETFs or required deep financial expertise to articulate custom theses.

By enabling investors to express their conviction in plain English—"invest in space exploration companies" or "semiconductor makers with 25%+ revenue growth"—AI agents could instantly research, filter thousands of securities, and assemble diversified indices tailored to each user's worldview.

Key Insight: Generated Assets transforms investment from a gatekept professional activity into an accessible, personalized endeavor—answering: What if anyone could build an investment portfolio as unique as their own conviction?

Navigating Challenges & Guardrails

⚖️ Risks & Regulatory Landscape 1 / 2

AI Hallucination & Model Risk

Hallucination Risk: AI agents may "hallucinate" stock research—citing non-existent earnings data or misinterpreting news articles. A user could build a portfolio based on fabricated fundamentals.

Backtest Correlation Breakdown: Historical backtests assume past correlations hold in the future. During market regime shifts (macro crisis, sector rotation), AI-selected portfolios could underperform dramatically.

Concentration Risk: Users might create overly concentrated "AI-selected" portfolios without understanding diversification limits. The system should enforce diversification requirements.

Mitigation Strategy: Clear disclaimers ("not investment advice"), human review layer on high-confidence recommendations, continuous model auditing, diversification enforcement, regular backtesting against new market regimes.

SEC/FINRA Oversight & Competition

Regulatory Scrutiny: SEC and FINRA are actively monitoring AI-driven investment advice. Generated Assets could face classification as an "investment adviser" requiring registration and compliance overhead.

Competitive Pressure: Schwab, Fidelity, and Vanguard are integrating AI into their platforms. These incumbents have regulatory relationships, massive user bases, and capital to compete.

Market Headwinds: Market volatility may dampen new user acquisition. During downturns, retail investors become more cautious—reducing demand for "personalized thematic indices."

Upside Opportunity: Despite risks, market trends strongly favor Generated Assets. Shift from passive indexing to conviction investing, rising retail sophistication, and GenAI adoption create a structural tailwind. Company is well-positioned to become the go-to platform for thematic, AI-assisted investing.
